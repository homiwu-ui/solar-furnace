Generate a 3D isometric view of a DIY wooden dual-axis solar tracker platform for a solar furnace, suitable for elementary school science fair.

The structure includes:
① Bottom base: 30x30cm wooden board with anti-slip pads at four corners
② Center shaft: M8 threaded rod (15cm long) fixed vertically at base center with nuts
③ Turntable: 25x25cm wooden board with 4x 608ZZ ball bearings embedded in a cross pattern near center (3cm from center, 90° apart), rotating on center shaft
④ Upright columns: Two 2x2x20cm wooden bars screwed to turntable, each with a 6mm hole at top
⑤ Solar furnace: Parabolic mirror assembly (18-segment acrylic) mounted between columns via M6 screw shafts as rotation axis, tiltable 0° to 80°
⑥ Horizontal servo motor: MG996R metal gear servo (9-10 kg·cm torque) mounted on base, with 10T pinion gear driving 60T gear on turntable bottom
⑦ Vertical servo motor: MG996R metal gear servo mounted on one upright column, connecting rod linked to furnace rotation shaft
⑧ Counterweight: ~300g weight hanging on back side of furnace via wire to balance vertical load
